Appear for the SAT – Undergraduate Entrance Exam / Scholastic Aptitude Test (IIIT-H)

The Undergraduate Entrance Examination (UGEE) is the entrance exam for IIIT Hyderabad (IIIT-H) for B.Tech programs. Applicants in this mode take a 3-hour computer-based UGEE at IIIT Hyderabad, consisting of two sections: SUPR – Subject Proficiency Test (60 mins) and REAP – Research Aptitude Test (120 mins). Candidates are shortlisted based on SUPR scores, prioritizing REAP scores, with 25% negative marking in each section.

SAT – Undergraduate Entrance Exam / Scholastic Aptitude Test (IIIT-H)

UGEE – Entry for B.Tech via IIIT-H Scholastic Aptitude Test

Conducted by:
IIIT Hyderabad

Eligibility:
Class 12 with PCM, 60% aggregate

Seats:
100–120 seats available

Application Fee:
₹3,000 (Males), ₹1,500 (Females)

Exam Month:
April (once a year)

Exam Mode:
CBT – SUPR (60 min) + REAP (120 min), 25% negative marking

Appearing Candidates:
30,000–60,000 students

Appear for the ACT – American College Testing

The ACT (American College Testing) is a standardized test used for college admissions in the United States and Canada. It assesses a student's readiness for college-level coursework by evaluating their knowledge and skills in English, mathematics, reading, and science.

ACT – American College Testing

Standardized Test for College Admissions

Conducted by:
ACT Inc.

Eligibility:
Open to all; common among high school students

Seats:
Available in India; fewer applicants than SAT

Application Fee:
₹4,835.25 (without Writing) / ₹6,754 (with Writing)

Exam Months:
Feb, Apr, Jun, Jul, Sep, Oct, Dec

Exam Mode:
English, Math, Reading, Science + Optional Writing

Appearing Candidates:
Over 1.4 million annually

Appear for the GRE – Graduate Record Examination

The GRE (Graduate Record Examination) is a standardized test used for graduate school admissions, primarily in the US and Canada. It assesses an applicant's aptitude in analytical writing, verbal reasoning, and quantitative reasoning, helping universities evaluate candidates' readiness for graduate studies.

GRE – Graduate Record Examination

Standardized Test for Graduate Admissions

Conducted by:
Educational Testing Service (ETS)

Eligibility:
Open to anyone; no specific qualifications or age limits required

Seats:
Accepted by over 1,300 business and graduate programs worldwide

Application Fee:
GRE General – ₹22,550
GRE Subject – ₹14,500

Exam Months:
April, September, October

Exam Mode:
Computer-based test covering Analytical Writing, Verbal, and Quantitative Reasoning

Appearing Candidates:
India leads with 1,13,304 test-takers, followed by the US and China

Appear for the GMAT – Graduate Management Admission Test

The GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test) is a standardized exam used by business schools to assess the skills needed for success in graduate management programs, particularly MBA programs. It evaluates a candidate's quantitative reasoning, verbal reasoning, and data analysis abilities.

GMAT – Graduate Management Admission Test

Entrance Exam for Global MBA Programs

Conducted by:
Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC)

Eligibility:
No specific criteria; open to all interested candidates

Seats:
Accepted by 7,700+ programs at 2,400+ business schools worldwide

Application Fee:
₹22,950 (Test center)
₹25,000 (Online proctored)

Exam Months:
Available year-round

Exam Mode:
Computer-based (Test center and Online proctored)

Appearing Candidates:
78,429 unique candidates (117,510 total registrations)

Appear for the IELTS – International English Language Testing System

The IELTS (International English Language Testing System) is a standardized test of English language proficiency used worldwide for academic and immigration purposes. IELTS is used by universities, employers, and immigration authorities to assess English language skills. IELTS is widely accepted by educational institutions, employers, and immigration authorities in English-speaking countries.

IELTS – International English Language Testing System

English Language Proficiency Test for Study, Work, and Migration

Conducted by:
British Council, IDP IELTS, and Cambridge University Press & Assessment

Eligibility:
Open to anyone aged 16 and above

Seats:
Accepted by all UK and Australian universities and 3,400+ U.S. institutions

Application Fee:
₹18,000 (Academic & General Training)

Exam Months:
Conducted year-round, multiple times each month

Exam Mode:
IELTS on Paper and IELTS on Computer

Appearing Candidates:
Taken by 4 million+ people annually worldwide

Appear for the TOEFL – Test of English as a Foreign Language

The TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language) is a standardized academic English test primarily taken by students applying to universities in the United States. The TOEFL is also accepted as proof of English ability by some universities outside the United States.

TOEFL – Test of English as a Foreign Language

Academic English Proficiency Test for Study Abroad

Conducted by:
Educational Testing Service (ETS)

Eligibility:
Open to anyone wishing to demonstrate English proficiency

Seats:
Accepted by 13,000+ institutions across 160+ countries

Application Fee:
₹16,900 (TOEFL iBT)

Exam Months:
Available year-round with monthly test dates

Exam Mode:
TOEFL iBT (Internet-Based Test)

Appearing Candidates:
Approximately 2.3 lakh candidates per year

Appear for the PTE – Pearson Test of English

The PTE Academic is a computer-based English language test that assesses reading, writing, speaking, and listening skills. PTE is accepted by thousands of universities, institutions, and governments worldwide, including in the UK, Australia, Canada, and New Zealand.

PTE – Pearson Test of English

Computer-based English Language Proficiency Test

Conducted by:
Pearson PLC Group

Eligibility:
Minimum age 16 (parental consent needed for 16–18). Open to all

Seats:
62 centers across India + global test network

Application Fee:
₹17,000 (including GST)

Exam Months:
Available year-round including April, May, June

Exam Mode:
Computer-based test: Speaking, Writing, Reading, Listening

Appearing Candidates:
~1.231 million candidates annually
