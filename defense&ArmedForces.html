Defense & Armed Forces Exams

Prepare for Success with NDA – National Defence Academy Exam

The National Defence Academy (NDA) Exam is a competitive entrance exam conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) twice a year to select cadets for training at the NDA and Indian Naval Academy (INA). It's a two-stage process: a written exam followed by an interview by the Services Selection Board (SSB).

NDA – National Defence Academy Exam

NDA – For Admission to National Defence Academy and Indian Naval Academy

Conducted by:
Union Public Service Commission (UPSC)

Eligibility:
Age: 16.5–19.5 years
Education: 10+2 or equivalent

Seats:
Total 406 (Army: 208, Navy: 42, Air Force: 120)

Application Fee:
₹100 (General/OBC)

Exam Months:
April and September

Exam Mode:
Pen and Paper-based

Appearing Candidates:
Over 2.4–3.5 lakh candidates

Serve the Nation with CDS – Combined Defence Services Exam

The Combined Defence Services (CDS) Examination is a national-level entrance exam conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) twice a year to recruit candidates for various academies within the Indian Armed Forces. It selects candidates for the Indian Military Academy (IMA), Indian Naval Academy (INA), Air Force Academy (AFA), and Officers' Training Academy (OTA).

CDS – Combined Defence Services Exam

CDS – For Recruitment into Indian Armed Forces Academies

Conducted by:
Union Public Service Commission (UPSC)

Eligibility:
Indian citizen, Bachelor's degree, Age 20–24 years

Seats:
457 vacancies across IMA, INA, AFA, OTA

Application Fee:
₹200 (General/OBC male candidates)

Exam Months:
April and September

Exam Mode:
Pen and Paper-based (Offline)

Appearing Candidates:
80k–1 lakh register; 40k–60k appear

Take Flight with AFCAT – Air Force Common Admission Test

The Air Force Common Admission Test (AFCAT) is an entrance exam for the Indian Air Force (IAF). It's held twice a year to recruit officers for flying and ground duty branches. AFCAT serves the purpose of selecting Class-I Gazetted Officers for the Indian Air Force.

AFCAT – Air Force Common Admission Test

AFCAT – Entry into Indian Air Force as Class-I Gazetted Officer

Conducted by:
Indian Air Force (IAF)

Eligibility:
Indian citizens, Age 20–24 (up to 26 with CPL), Degree/Engineering with 60%

Seats:
336 vacancies (AFCAT 1 2025)

Application Fee:
₹550 (including GST)

Exam Months:
February and August

Exam Mode:
Computer-Based Test (MCQ format)

Appearing Candidates:
~2 lakh appear annually

Prepare for the Rigorous SSB Interview – Services Selection Board Interview

The SSB (Services Selection Board) interview is conducted by the Indian Ministry of Defence to assess candidates for officer positions in the Indian Armed Forces. The SSB interview is a comprehensive five-day evaluation process that includes screening tests, psychological assessments, group tasks, and a final conference.

SSB Interview – Services Selection Board Interview

SSB – Gateway to Officer Positions in the Indian Armed Forces

Conducted by:
Ministry of Defence, India

Eligibility:
Must qualify NDA, CDS, AFCAT, or direct entries

Seats:
Around 700–1000 selected annually

Application Fee:
No application fee

Interview Schedule:
2–3 months after written results

Process:
5-day assessment: tests, tasks, interview

Appearing Candidates:
~7800–8200 candidates called annually

Appear for the Written Admission Test (WAT) – Army Welfare Education Society (AWES)

The AWES (Army Welfare Education Society) uses a Written Admission Test (WAT) or Online Screening Test (OST) for recruitment, primarily for teaching positions (PGT, TGT, PRT) and admission to professional colleges/institutes, assessing candidates' knowledge and pedagogical skills.

Written Admission Test (WAT) – Army Welfare Education Society (AWES)

WAT – Entry Gateway for Teaching and Professional Courses under AWES

Conducted by:
Agency identified by HQ AWES

Eligibility:
Bachelor's degree with 50%+ marks

Seats:
Varies by course and institution

Application Fee:
₹385/-

Exam Month:
November (once a year)

Exam Type:
WAT/OST (Online Screening Test)

Appearing Candidates:
2.3 lakh+ students involved
